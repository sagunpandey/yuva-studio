# Deck Building — The Game Within the Game

**Dominion** changed everything in 2008. Before it, decks were built before the game started. Dominion made building your deck *the game itself* — and spawned an entire genre.

## The Core Loop

A deck builder works like this:
1. Start with a weak, inefficient starting deck
2. Buy better cards from a shared market
3. Shuffle your new cards into your deck
4. Draw and play cards each turn
5. Repeat — your deck grows more powerful over time

The elegance is in the tension between **buying good cards** and **diluting your deck** with more cards to shuffle through.

## What Makes a Great Deck Builder

### Card Synergy
The best deck builders reward players who identify combos. A card that doubles your gold means nothing alone — but paired with cards that generate gold, it's devastating.

### Meaningful Choices at the Market
If every card is equally good, buying decisions are shallow. Great deck builders make the market feel like a puzzle where the optimal buy changes every turn.

### Thin Deck vs. Big Deck
A disciplined deck builder knows: **fewer cards = more consistency**. Drawing your best cards every turn requires resisting the urge to buy everything.

## Notable Deck Builders

| Game | Twist |
|------|-------|
| Dominion | The original. Pure and elegant. |
| Clank! | Board movement + deck building |
| Star Realms | 2-player head-to-head combat |
| Aeon's End | No shuffle mechanic — order matters |
| Thunderstone Advance | Dungeon delving integrated into buying |

## Deck Building in Hybrid Games

Many modern games incorporate deck building as *one* mechanic among many — **Gloomhaven**, **Mage Knight**, **Arkham Horror: The Card Game**. The deck becomes a personal engine within a larger strategic context.

---

Deck building succeeds because it gives every player a unique, evolving experience within a shared rule system. Your deck is your strategy made visible.

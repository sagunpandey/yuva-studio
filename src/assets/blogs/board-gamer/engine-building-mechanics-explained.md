# Engine Building Mechanics Explained

Engine building is one of the most satisfying mechanics in modern board gaming. You start small, build up your engine, and by the end of the game — it runs itself.

## The Core Loop

An engine building game follows a simple principle:
1. Start with limited resources and actions
2. Acquire cards, tiles, or abilities that generate more of what you need
3. Chain those abilities together until your engine runs efficiently

Games like **Wingspan**, **Terraforming Mars**, and **Gizmos** all execute this beautifully.

## Why Players Love It

There's a deep psychological satisfaction in watching a system you've built operate smoothly. It mirrors the joy of real-world engineering — design something that works better over time.

### The Snowball Effect
Good engine builders reward early investment. The more you build, the more you produce, and the more you can build. This snowball creates a sense of momentum.

### The Puzzle Element
Each game is a unique puzzle. With limited card pools and turn counts, players must figure out which combinations create the most efficient engine for that specific game.

## Common Pitfalls in Design

- **Runaway leaders** — once an engine is ahead, it's hard to catch up
- **Analysis paralysis** — too many options slow the game down
- **Shallow engines** — engines that don't feel synergistic miss the satisfaction

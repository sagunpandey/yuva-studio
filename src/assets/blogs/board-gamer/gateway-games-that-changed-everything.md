# Gateway Games That Changed Everything

Every board gamer remembers their first "real" game — the one that showed them there was a whole world beyond Monopoly and Scrabble. Gateway games are the front door to modern board gaming.

## What Makes a Gateway Game

A gateway game must:
- Teach quickly (under 15 minutes of rules explanation)
- Resolve in under 90 minutes
- Offer meaningful decisions without overwhelming new players
- Create memorable moments that make people want to play again

## The Hall of Fame

### Catan
The game that arguably brought Euro-style design to mainstream audiences in the 90s. Trading, negotiation, and resource management — but presented in a way anyone can grasp in one playthrough.

### Ticket to Ride
Route building with gentle competition and almost no conflict. Perfect for families and non-gamers. The rules fit on one page.

### Pandemic
Cooperative mechanics made accessible. Players work together against the game itself — no player elimination, no kingmaking, just shared problem-solving.

### Codenames
Proved that party games could be clever. Two-word clues, team deduction, and moments of pure linguistic creativity. Endlessly replayable.

### Wingspan
Brought engine-building to a massive audience with beautiful production and an accessible card structure. Proved that complexity can feel gentle.

## Why Gateway Games Matter

The board game hobby needs entry points. When a gateway game works, it doesn't just entertain — it creates a lifelong gamer.

The best gateway games don't feel like stepping stones. They feel like complete experiences on their own.

---

If you're introducing someone to modern board games, start with a gateway. The hobby will do the rest.
